# Weekly Content Calendar

> **Usage:** `/happyceo:draft-post` automatically applies the day's theme. Override by specifying a different format.

---

## Mandatory Days

| Day | Theme | Format | Tone |
|-----|-------|--------|------|
| **Monday** | Week Ahead / Goals | Short, punchy list of what you're working on | Energetic, focused |
| **Wednesday** | Hot Take / Opinion | Single strong point with reasoning | Direct, confident |
| **Friday** | Field Notes | Numbered observations from the week's experiments | Casual, reflective |

---

## Optional Days (If Time Permits)

| Day | Theme | Format | Tone |
|-----|-------|--------|------|
| **Tuesday** | Tutorial / How-To | Step-by-step or "here's how I did X" | Educational, practical |
| **Thursday** | Case Study / Win | Client story, feature shipped, problem solved | Story-driven, specific |
| **Saturday** | Long-form / Vision | Thesis, analysis, where things are heading | Thoughtful, expansive |
| **Sunday** | Personal / Community | Local event, build in public update | Warm, human |

---

## Format Details

### Monday: Week Ahead
- 3-5 bullet points of what you're focused on
- Can include goals, meetings, builds
- Short — under 500 characters
- Energy: "Here's what's cooking this week"

### Wednesday: Hot Take
- One clear opinion or observation
- Back it up with brief reasoning or example
- Invites discussion/debate
- Medium length — 500-800 characters

### Friday: Field Notes
- Numbered list (3-7 points)
- Observations, experiments, learnings from the week
- Casual tone
- Tag people/venues involved
- Reflective: "Here's what I noticed this week"

### Saturday: Long-form / Vision
- Deeper analysis or thesis
- Lighter version of a blog post
- Industry trends, predictions, frameworks
- 1000-1500 characters

---

## Override Behavior

If you're drafting on a Friday but want a different format:
- Specify in your request: "draft a hot take about X"
- Or say "ignore the calendar, this is a tutorial"
